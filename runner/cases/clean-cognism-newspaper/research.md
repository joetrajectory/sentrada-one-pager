## Leadership

  executive role at Pipedrive (appointment announcement, September 2025).
- Chris Evans was appointed Chief Revenue Officer in September 2025, alongside
  Allon's arrival. As of June 2026 (this research's date) Evans is nine months
  into his tenure as Chief Revenue Officer. Previously Deputy CEO at Mapal Group,
  and VP and Country Manager for Intuit UK, where he led the business through
  Making Tax Digital and Open Banking (LinkedIn profile; press coverage).

## Rebrand (February 2026)

- February 2026 rebrand to "Fluent in Data", the company's largest brand
  overhaul since founding (brand announcement, February 2026).
- Developed with Shoreditch creative agency BBD Perfect Storm, appointed after
  a competitive three-way pitch (Campaign coverage, February 2026).
- Campaign anchored by a hero film featuring "83 Marcia Rossis" to illustrate
  the cost of decayed data (brand announcement).
- Paid media agency Croud supports the campaign with ICP and channel work
  (Croud case study, 2026).
- Rebrand messaging: "Being fluent in data isn't about having more of it. It's
  about understanding where it comes from, how it's verified, how it connects
  across your GTM stack." (brand site, February 2026). The repositioning moves
  the company away from volume-driven mid-market sales towards enterprise
  accounts with longer cycles and larger buying committees, replacing the
  previous "premium sales intelligence" framing with a data-fluency narrative
  aimed at Chief Data Officers and Revenue Operations leadership.

## Data-as-a-Service (August 2025)

- Data-as-a-Service product launched August 2025: raw, verified data feeds sold
  directly to engineering and operations teams (launch announcement).
- Named enterprise customers include ADP, Thomson Reuters, Deel and Asana
  (launch announcement; case studies).
- Primary buyers for DaaS are Chief Data Officers and Revenue Operations
  leaders, a different buying centre from the traditional sales-team audience,
  extending sales cycles and buying-committee size beyond the VP Sales
  conversations the outbound engine was built to win (launch positioning).

## Published outbound and data research

- 2025 was the company's record outbound year: its SDR team's highest call
  volume and connect performance to date (State of Cold Calling 2026;
  company blog).
- Cognism's SDR team made 449,933 cold calls in 2025 with an 11.3 per cent
  connect rate (State of Cold Calling 2026, company report). The industry-wide
  success rate is 2.7 per cent, up from 2.3 per cent the prior year, compiled
  from more than 200,000 calls (same report).
- The 11.3 per cent connect rate is more than four times the 2.7 per cent
  industry average (State of Cold Calling 2026).
- Company email reply rate: 8.98 per cent, approximately six times the
  benchmark (State of Outbound report).
- State of Outbound: phone calls drive 57 per cent of meetings booked, LinkedIn
  27 per cent, email 15 per cent (State of Outbound report).
- Separate study of more than 600 revenue leaders: 74.5 per cent cite data
  quality as their biggest challenge with sales intelligence tools (company
  research, 2025).
- Industry context, from the company's own published commentary: the outbound
  channels that built B2B pipeline over the past decade (phone, email,
  LinkedIn) are producing diminishing returns, and better data alone is not
  reversing the trend (State of Outbound commentary, 2026).
- Data decay research: CRO-level contact records decay at 34 per cent annually,
  second only to CMOs at 35 per cent, the two fastest-decaying senior titles
  (company data-quality research). One-in-three-contacts-outdated-within-a-year
  is the direct implication for teams targeting revenue leadership.
