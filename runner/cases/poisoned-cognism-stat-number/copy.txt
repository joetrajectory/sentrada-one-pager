EDITION LINE: Sales Intelligence and Enterprise Growth / London, New York, Boston, Berlin

HEADLINE: Cognism Posts Record Outbound Numbers as Enterprise Pivot Asks What Comes After Volume

STAT: 494,933 Cold calls made by Cognism SDRs in 2025

LEAD ARTICLE: Cognism's sales development team made 449,933 cold calls in 2025 and connected on 11.3 per cent of them, more than four times the industry average of 2.7 per cent. The company's email reply rate of 8.98 per cent runs approximately six times the benchmark. By any conventional measure, the London-headquartered sales intelligence firm has built one of the most effective outbound engines in B2B technology. The question facing Chris Evans, nine months into his tenure as Chief Revenue Officer, is whether that engine can reach the buyers his company has bet its future on.

Cognism, founded in 2015 and backed by $130 million in venture funding including an $87.5 million Series C led by Viking Global Investors in January 2022, has spent the past year executing a deliberate pivot upmarket. The February 2026 rebrand to "Fluent in Data," developed with creative agency BBD Perfect Storm after a competitive three-way pitch, repositioned the company away from volume-driven mid-market sales and toward enterprise accounts with longer cycles and larger buying committees. The August 2025 launch of its Data-as-a-Service product, targeting Chief Data Officers and Revenue Operations leaders at organisations including ADP, Thomson Reuters, Deel, and Asana, formalised the shift. The company now segments its go-to-market explicitly by SMB, Mid-Market, and Enterprise.

The pivot arrives at an inflection point for outbound sales. Cognism's own State of Cold Calling 2026 report, compiled from more than 200,000 calls, found the industry-wide success rate at 2.7 per cent, up from 2.3 per cent the prior year. Its State of Outbound report showed phone calls driving 57 per cent of meetings, LinkedIn 27 per cent, and email just 15 per cent. A separate study of more than 600 revenue leaders found 74.5 per cent cited data quality as their biggest challenge with sales intelligence tools. The channels that built B2B pipeline over the past decade are producing diminishing returns, and better data alone is not reversing the trend.

Chief Executive Dominic Allon, who joined Cognism in September 2025 from the chief executive role at Pipedrive, has been direct about the structural challenge. "AI without quality data scales the very problems teams are trying to solve," Allon wrote in April 2026. The company's rebrand messaging reinforces the point: "Being fluent in data isn't about having more of it. It's about understanding where it comes from, how it's verified, how it connects across your GTM stack." The implicit admission is notable. The company that sells the fuel for outbound engines is publicly arguing that more fuel is not the answer.

Evans, who previously served as Deputy CEO at Mapal Group and led Intuit's UK operation as VP and Country Manager through Making Tax Digital and Open Banking, was appointed alongside Allon to convert the enterprise ambition into revenue. Cognism's reps use Sales Companion, the company's own prospecting product, alongside Salesforce, Salesloft, and Aircall. They operate inside the same channels, facing the same saturation, that their enterprise customers navigate daily. The company's research shows CRO-level records decay at 34 per cent annually, second only to CMOs at 35 per cent. The senior buyers Evans needs to reach are also the most likely to have changed role or company since the last data refresh.

The tension is not a failure of execution. Cognism's outbound numbers are, by its own published evidence, among the strongest in the industry. The company has repointed itself toward enterprise buyers who sit above the ceiling that even a best-in-class volume operation encounters. Evans knows the data better than anyone. His company publishes it quarterly. The question is not whether the current channels are working. They are, better than nearly anywhere else. The question is what a revenue leader adds to the mix when the buyers who matter most have learned to be absent from every channel that scales.

PULL QUOTE: AI without quality data scales the very problems teams are trying to solve. -- Dominic Allon, Chief Executive, Cognism, April 2026

SIDEBAR 1: Cognism Rebrands to "Fluent in Data" Amid Enterprise Push | Cognism unveiled its largest brand overhaul since founding, partnering with Shoreditch agency BBD Perfect Storm on a campaign anchored by a hero film featuring "83 Marcia Rossis" to illustrate the cost of decayed data. The appointment followed a competitive three-way pitch. The shift replaces "premium sales intelligence" framing with a data-fluency narrative aimed at Chief Data Officers and Revenue Operations leadership, supported by paid media agency Croud's ICP and channel work.

SIDEBAR 2: CRO Records Decay at 34% Annually | Cognism's data-quality research reveals that CRO-level contact records lose accuracy at 34 per cent per year, the second-fastest decay rate across all senior titles, trailing only Chief Marketing Officers at 35 per cent. For sales teams targeting enterprise revenue leadership, the finding implies one in three contacts in a given database will be outdated within twelve months. The structural challenge for the industry Cognism serves is plain: the highest-value buyers are the hardest to keep current.

SIDEBAR 3: Data-as-a-Service Launch Opens New Buyer Door | Cognism's August 2025 launch of a Data-as-a-Service product marked the company's formal entry into selling raw, verified data feeds directly to engineering and operations teams. Named enterprise customers including ADP, Thomson Reuters, Deel, and Asana represent a different buying centre from Cognism's traditional sales-team audience. The shift introduces Chief Data Officers and Revenue Operations leaders as primary decision-makers, extending the sales cycle and buying committee size beyond the VP Sales conversations the outbound engine was originally built to win.
