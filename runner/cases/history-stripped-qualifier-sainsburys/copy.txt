EDITION LINE: Grocery Retail and Colleague Strategy / London, Coventry, Milton Keynes

HEADLINE: Sainsbury's £1.6bn Savings Programme Enters Its Decisive Year

STAT: 148,000 colleagues across the group

LEAD ARTICLE: Sainsbury's heads into the final year of its Next Level plan with food volume growth ahead of the market and its £1.6bn cost saving programme on track for March 2027. Launched in February 2024, the plan has moved general merchandise into food stores and pushed the Argos integration through its hardest phase.

"Our focus on food is winning customers from every one of our competitors," Simon Roberts said in April 2026. The question for the group's 148,000 colleagues is what the plan asks of them in its decisive year.

PULL QUOTE: Our focus on food is winning customers from every one of our competitors. -- Simon Roberts, Chief Executive, Sainsbury's, April 2026

SIDEBAR 1: Next Level Plan Enters Final Stretch | The strategy launched in February 2024 reaches its decisive year with the cost programme of over £1.6bn targeted for completion by March 2027, and food volume growth still running ahead of the market on Kantar's read.

SIDEBAR 2: Argos Moves Deeper Into Food Stores | The integration of Argos and the shift of general merchandise into food stores remains a named strand of the plan, consolidating the group's store estate around its strongest format.

SIDEBAR 3: Support Centres Carry the Plan | Beyond the Holborn headquarters, the group's major support centres in Coventry and Milton Keynes carry much of the programme's execution load, from range resets to the systems work behind them.
